I see you're here to learn about my Price Comparison Web Scraper, Alex's Tools.

The App

Alex's Tools is a price comparison web scraping application that allows users to search and track the prices of their favorite products from sites such as Amazon.com and HomeDepot.com. Users can opt to receive email alerts when items that they wish to track drop in price, allowing them to purchase their items for the best deal.

The Inspiration

I came up with the application idea when I was spending time with a close friend--Alex. He's an apprentice electrician who is always looking at new tools. He spent so much time switching between websites, trying to find the best prices, shipping deals, and coupon codes. I knew this process could be more straightforward, and I wanted to find a way to make that a reality. After all, doesn't technology exist to solve problems and make life easier? So I went into the drafting stage.

The Challenges

The first challenge was figuring out the structure and design of the application: What framework would I use? What are my use cases? What will set my application apart from other existing apps and extensions? I spent a long time in this drafting stage, leading me to think outside the box and anticipate my audience's needs. Next, I faced the challenge of developing the code for the application, designing and implementing my UI, and testing and editing constantly. At times, I felt like a jack of all trades, trying to do a little bit of everything since I was the only one working on the project, and other times, I felt like the master of none. However, the frustration in the latter experiences fueled my fire for growth and helped me learn new skills and improve old ones. This project has indeed been a unique creative experience.

Future of Alex's Tools

Some features that I would like to implement are a user sign-in feature to allow users to keep track of their purchases and manage their notification settings. Additionally, I would like for my application to be able to handle data from a greater selection of websites. Ideally, this application would one day hold a variety of products in its database already, thus allowing users to search the product list of scraped data as opposed to entering a link to scrape the data.
